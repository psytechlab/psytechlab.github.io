357 days have passed since the post where we congratulated everyone on the upcoming current year. Back then we created Psytechlab. Let's see what we've accomplished during this time.

First and foremost, we created "Whaleboy", our anti-suicide platform that had been in development for four years. We're not ashamed to say that it turned out to be a whole ecosystem, because "Whaleboy" is not just a platform - it's also a dataset, models, and a bunch of satellite programs.

We presented at venues with developers (Python Meetup, ODS Data Fest) and psychologists (CBT FORUM 2025, Open Doors.CONF). Our "agents" (meaning people) are present both in Moscow and St. Petersburg. The presentations are bearing fruit: we've partnered with the "Open Doors" project - a platform for accessible psychological help. Together with them, we're developing a training bot for psychologists. We showed the demo version at the October conference, where we received our first feedback.

We translated several dialogue datasets from the psychology domain using our translation pipeline. We continue working on it: learning to identify poor translations and account for cultural specifics. By the way, we developed a unified format for the datasets themselves.

Besides presentations, we launched a website and Telegram channel where we unobtrusively but regularly shared our current activities. We even got our first visual identity.

On the scientific front, we published an article about our dataset, as well as two abstracts on the translation pipeline. By the way, a student did the main part of the work and wrote the abstracts herself. Unexpectedly, another student found us, wanting to dedicate her master's thesis to our suicide topic - she'll be looking at how modern LLMs handle this subject. So we're not just a scientific team, but also an educational one.

By the way, if you happen to be a student looking for a topic for a course project or thesis, or you simply want to contribute something to the team, we have something for you. We really want to learn how to split complex sentences in Russian into component parts. For unknown reasons, there are no properly working methods for this task. All that needs to be done is annotate a dataset and train/test different models. If you're interested, write to us at psytechlab24@gmail.com.

Now about plans for the upcoming year.

You might be wondering why we translated so many datasets? Here's the answer - we want to create a psychological benchmark in Russian: what models know about psychology and therapy and how well they can empathize. Without metrics, we're like in a fog. Yes, some of our datasets will be translated, but that's better than nothing at all. Moreover, we'll further refine the translation.

We're also preparing scientific articles on synthetic data for the psychology domain. We briefly talked about them in the previous devlog. We'll present experimental results there and a small mathematical model for analyzing synthetic data quality.

A bit more about identity and branding. We realized that "PsyTechLab" is very hard on the eyes (and inconvenient to type), so we'll be simply "Psytechlab" everywhere, as we have been throughout this year. We also need to create a logo, since our cat picture is really more about mood.

We have a scattering of other ideas that we're developing and thinking about which one to take on first. Stay with us to learn about them.
